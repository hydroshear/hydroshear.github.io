HydroShear: Hydroelastic Shear Simulation for Tactile Sim-to-Real Reinforcement Learning

Anonymous Authors
Code (coming soon)

TL;DR HydroShear is a non-holonomic hydroelastic model for high-fidelity tactile shear simulation for training zero-shot sim-to-real tactile reinforcement learning policies on diverse contact and force-rich manipulation tasks.




Abstract

Sim-to-real transfer for contact-rich manipulation requires sensing modalities that go beyond geometry to capture the dynamics of interaction. Existing simulators for vision-based tactile sensors primarily focus on rendering images and either neglect tactile shear or approximate it with oversimplified models, leading to a persistent sim-to-real gap for many dexterous tasks. We present HydroShear, a non-holonomic hydroelastic tactile shear simulation framework that models path-dependent shear fields for various tactile sensors including vision-based and magnetic-based. HydroShear extends hydroelastic contact models using Signed Distance Functions (SDFs) to track object paths across the sensor membrane, enabling precise simulation of shear force fields and twist during complex sticking and slipping events. This approach generates physics-based, computationally efficient force fields from arbitrary watertight geometries while remaining agnostic to the underlying physics engine. In experiments with a GelSight Mini sensor, HydroShear more faithfully reproduces real tactile shear than existing methods and enables zero-shot sim-to-real transfer of reinforcement learning policies for insertion and fine gripper control for slippages, achieving substantially higher success rates than prior tactile shear simulators.

Experimental Results


We train tactile-based RL policies in simulation and test under zero-shot sim-to-real transfer.
We compare our method HydroShear against state-of-the-art baselines TacSL and FOTS.
We evaluate the policies four tasks: Peg Insertion, Bin Packing, Book Shelving, and Drawer Pulling.
